Cave 16 – The Majestic Mahayana Masterpiece

Ajanta Cave 16, dating to the 5th century CE under the patronage of Varahadeva, is one of the most significant caves in the entire complex. It is a large and elaborately decorated Mahayana vihara with a spacious hall, about 15 monk cells, and a grand shrine featuring a seated Buddha in pralambapadasana (legs-down posture), flanked by richly adorned Padmapani and Vajrapani Bodhisattvas.

What sets Cave 16 apart is its world-famous murals, especially the iconic Padmapani Bodhisattva painting, which showcases advanced techniques of shading, realism, and spiritual depth. The murals depict Jataka tales, royal court scenes, and Buddhist teachings. The intricate carvings, sculptural richness, and detailed frescoes make Cave 16 a spiritual, architectural, and artistic landmark of Indian Buddhist heritage.
