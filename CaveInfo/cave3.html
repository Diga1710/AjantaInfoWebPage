Information About Cave 3

Cave 3 – The Abandoned Monastery

Cave 3, also known as the Abandoned Monastery, is less ornate than its neighbors but holds significant historical value. It features a large prayer hall with a stupa at the rear, surrounded by monastic cells. The simplicity of its design reflects the austere lifestyle of the monks who once inhabited it.

Though devoid of decorative elements, its archaeological significance is high. It reveals early quarrying techniques, incomplete chisel marks, and planning strategies of ancient cave excavators. Cave 3 stands as a raw, behind-the-scenes look at the practical and material limitations encountered during Ajanta’s golden age of construction.
