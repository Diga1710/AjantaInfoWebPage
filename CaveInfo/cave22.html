Information About Cave 22

Cave 22 – A Small Vihara for Individual Worship

Cave 22 is a simple and unfinished vihara, possibly used for solitary retreat and worship. It contains a small hall with a single cell and a shrine, where a Buddha is seated in dhyana mudra (meditation posture). The surrounding walls are bare, with no murals or decoration.

Though modest in scale, Cave 22 offers insight into the introspective aspect of Buddhist practice—solitude, meditation, and simplicity. The cave likely served a single monk or a very small group. Its presence among the larger and more elaborate caves shows the diversity of spiritual needs within the monastic complex.
