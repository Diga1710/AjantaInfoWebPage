Information About Cave 10

Cave 10 – The Oldest Painted Chaitya

Cave 10 is the oldest cave at Ajanta, dating to the 2nd century BCE, and it’s also a chaitya-griha. Its design includes a central nave with side aisles formed by 39 pillars and a prominent stupa at the rear. The vaulted ceiling includes carved stone imitating wooden beams, showing architectural evolution from wood to rock.

Significantly, Cave 10 contains India’s earliest surviving mural paintings, though most are faded. These include Jataka tales and floral designs in tempera, offering a glimpse into the storytelling methods that would flourish in later Mahayana caves. As a space for group worship and chantings, Cave 10 bridges early ascetic practice with emerging artistic expression.
