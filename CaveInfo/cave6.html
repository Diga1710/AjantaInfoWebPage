Information About Cave 6

Cave 6 – The Two-Storied Sanctuary of Innovation

Cave 6 is Ajanta’s only double-storeyed monastery, demonstrating advanced architectural experimentation. Its lower level was begun around 460 CE, with the upper added later—possibly around 465 CE after Cave 5 was abandoned. The lower floor includes a large hall, shrine with Buddha in teaching posture, and extensive narrative reliefs like the Shravasti Miracle and Mara’s Temptation.

The upper level, though incomplete, includes additional prayer chambers and another shrine. Notably, the upper Buddha image is accompanied by a kneeling devotee—an early example of personalized worship. The cave features rich carvings of divine beings, animals, and narrative motifs. Despite its hurried execution and partial finish, Cave 6 symbolizes a pivotal moment in Mahayana devotion and spatial complexity.
