Information About Cave 12

Cave 12 – Early Theravāda Vihara of Simplicity

Cave 12 is one of the oldest Hinayana viharas at Ajanta, dating to the 2nd century BCE. It reflects the earliest phase of Buddhist rock-cut architecture, built purely for residential and meditative purposes. The cave has a plain rectangular hall, three monk cells at the rear, and no decorative features or Buddha shrine.

There are no sculptures, murals, or iconographic elements, in keeping with the Theravāda emphasis on renunciation and inward contemplation. Despite its visual simplicity, Cave 12 is structurally intact and offers valuable historical context for understanding the practical, ascetic life of early Buddhist monastics.
