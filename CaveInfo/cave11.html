Cave 11 – A Modest Vihara with Mahayana Influence

Cave 11 is a small vihara from the 5th–6th century CE and represents a transitional form of Buddhist architecture. Though simple in layout, it reflects the shift toward Buddha iconography. The cave consists of a rectangular hall with side monk cells, stone benches, and a rear sanctum containing a seated Buddha in dhyana mudra.

Some traces of mural paintings remain on the walls and ceiling, with faint floral patterns and donor figures. This cave shows how devotional practices were increasingly integrated into functional monastic residences during the Mahayana period, blending simplicity with the emerging role of icons in worship.
