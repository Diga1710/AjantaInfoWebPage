Information About Cave 9

Cave 9 – One of the Oldest Chaitya Halls

Cave 9 is a chaitya-griha or prayer hall from the 2nd century BCE, belonging to the early Hinayana period. It features a vaulted nave, side aisles divided by 23 octagonal pillars, and a prominent stone stupa at the apsidal end, intended for ritual circumambulation. The stupa served as a symbolic presence of the Buddha, not a sculpted image.

Though primarily Hinayana in design, the cave was later retouched during the Mahayana period with faded murals depicting Jataka tales and donor figures. The cave’s layout, wooden beam-like stone ceiling, and preserved structure make it a key example of early Buddhist congregational architecture transitioning into a more visual storytelling tradition.
