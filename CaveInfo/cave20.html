Information About Cave 20

Cave 20 – A Modest Monastic Cave with Simplicity

Ajanta Cave 20 is a small vihara built during the Mahayana phase and located next to the grand Cave 19. Though not elaborately decorated, it features a square hall, three monk cells, and a sanctum with a seated Buddha image. The Buddha is depicted in a meditative posture, symbolizing inner peace and enlightenment.

The cave lacks extensive ornamentation, and there are no murals or complex carvings, but the doorway and sanctum frame show some decorative elements. Cave 20 emphasizes the spiritual function of monastic spaces, supporting meditation and devotion without excessive embellishment. It served as a practical living and worship area for monks.
