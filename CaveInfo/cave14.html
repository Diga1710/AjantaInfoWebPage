Cave 14 – An Incomplete Mahayana Vihara

Cave 14, located just above Cave 13, is an unfinished Mahayana vihara from the 5th century CE. Though incomplete, its architectural plan indicates a larger and more decorative structure was envisioned. The cave includes a wide hall, partially excavated monk cells, and space for a central shrine, though the shrine was never completed. The partially carved pillars and early attempts at decorative motifs reflect the Mahayana intent of combining residence with ritual function.

Traces of Bodhisattva figures near the entrance and incomplete interior carvings suggest the plan for rich iconography. Cave 14 marks a transition from early Hinayana austerity to the Mahayana focus on devotion and symbolism, even though the final artistic vision was never realized.
